A determinate state is the state of a particle where repeated measurement of an observable $Q$ gives the same values. An example of this would be the stationary states of the Hamiltonian, where any measurement on $\Psi _{n}$ gives the energy $E_{n}$. The uncertainty of $Q$ in its determinate state would then be zero.
$$
\sigma_{Q}^{2}=\langle (Q-\langle Q \rangle )^{2} \rangle=\braket{ \Psi | (\hat{Q}-q)^{2}\Psi } =\braket{ (\hat{Q}-q)\Psi | (\hat{Q}-q)\Psi } =0
$$
here, $q=\langle Q \rangle$, we used the fact that $\hat{Q}-q$ is a hermitian operator. An inner product with oneself is zero only when the vector is zero. Then,
$$
\begin{gather*}
\hat{Q}\Psi=q\Psi
\end{gather*}
$$
This is an eigenvalue equation for the linear operator $\hat{Q}$ with eigenvalue $q$. We conclude that the determinate states of an observable $Q$ are eigenfunctions of the hermitian operator $\hat{Q}$.
