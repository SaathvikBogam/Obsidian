C. elegans is a nematode used to study animal development and cell differentiation. It is simple, with only 959 somatic cells and 1000-2000 germ cells. Its transparent body allows for vivo imaging of cellular and somatic processes.
Size: 1 mm in length, 80 $\mu$m in diameter
Lifespan: 2-3 weeks
Genome: 100M base pairs, 19000 genes
##### Applications
- Cell division and development
- Neurobiology
- Aging and longevity
- Gene function and expression
