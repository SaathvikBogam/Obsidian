If you measure any observable $Q(x,p)$ on a particle in the state $\Psi(x,t)$, you are certain to get an eigenvalue of the operator $\hat{Q}(x,-i\hbar \partial_{x})$. If the spectrum of $\hat{Q}$ is discrete, the probability of measuring an eigenvalue $q_{n}$ associated with an orthonormalized eigenfunction $f_{n}(x)$ is 
$$
P(q_{n})=|c_{n}|^{2},\ \text{where }c_{n}=\braket{ f_{n} | \Psi }
$$
If the spectrum is continuous with real eigenvalues $q(z)$ and associated (Dirac) orthonormalized eigenfunctions $f_{z}(x)$, the probability of getting a result in the range $dz$ is,
$$
|c(z)|^{2}dz,\ \text{where }c(z)=\braket{ f_{z} | \Psi }
$$
