(a) The given funcction is $f(x)=x^{\nu}$. If $f\in \mathcal{H}$, then its square integral on $(0,1)$ must converge.
$$
\int_{0}^{1} x^{\nu} \, dx =\frac{x^{\nu+1}}{\nu+1}\bigg|_{0}^{1}<\infty\ (\nu\neq -1)
$$
The integral diverges for $\nu<-1$. Therefore the $f(x)=x^{\nu}\in \mathcal{H}$ for $\nu\geq-1$.
