Centrifugation uses centrifugal force to seperate particles from a solution based on size, density, viscosity and rotor speed. The denser components move away from the centrifuge's axis and the less dense ones move toward it. The centrifuge uses the sedimentation principle.
#### Differrntial Centrifugation
- Sorts based on size, and is the simplest type
- Used to seperate organelles in a cell
- Large particles spin faster than smaller particles, and the rate of sedimentation can be increased by increasing the centrifugal force.
- Also known as Differential Velocity Centrifugation
- A tissue is first homogenized and lysed to break down cell membranes and mix cell contents and the homogenate is centrifuged.
- Large particles form a pellet at the bottom, leaving smaller particles.
- It is performed multiple times after removing the pellet and increasing centrifugal force.
#### Density Gradient Centrifugation
- Density gradient centrifugation seperates particles by density in an analte mixture.
- The substances are concentrated in a solution of caesium salts or sucrose. -
- It involves particle fractionation based on buoyant density.
- Molecules settle until they reach a medium of same density. As the sample rotates, denser one settle down.
- They suspend at densities that match the medium, allwing seperation into distinct layers for recovery.
- It is used to purify large volumes of biomolecules and different viruses. It can also be used to determine densities.
- This method was first used by the famous experiment which proved DNA is semi conservative using different isotopes of Nitrogen.
