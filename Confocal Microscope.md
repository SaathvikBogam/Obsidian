Confocal microscopy uses optical methods to obtain images from a specific focal plane. It collects a series of images through vertical depth, from which a 3D representation is generated. A laser beam reflects off a dichroic mirror, bounces on two scanning mirrors, then passes through the objective lens to illuminate a spot. The specimen's fluorescence passes through the objective, reflects off scanning mirrors and then onto dichroic mirror. This allows light to pass through the pinhole, blocking out of focus light, all light reaching photomultiplier is from focal spot. A computer then reconstructs the image.
