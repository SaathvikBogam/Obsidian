The principle of a fluorescence microscope is the Stokes shift $-$ difference in $\lambda$ between peak absorption and emmision (fluorescene) of a molecule. It occurs because molecules release energy as heat before emitting a photon, making emitted light have longer wavelengths/lower frequencies.

Light passes through an excitation filter to select a wavelength that excites fluorescent dye. A dichroic mirror then deflects the excitation light to the specimen. Fluorescent light is emitted by the specimen and then passes through a dichroic mirror and second filter to select the wavelength.
