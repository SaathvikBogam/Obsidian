There were three major advances that give us our knowledge about protein biosynthesis:
1. In 1950s, radioactive amino acids were injected into rats and after different time intervals, they removed the liver, homogenized it, fractionated it by centrifugation and examined subcellular fractions for presence of radioactive protein.
2. People found that amino acids were activated for protein synthesis when incubated with ATP and cytosolic fraction of liver cells. Amino acids became attached to heat stable soluble tRNA to form aminoacyl-tRNAs.
3. Genetic information that was in 4-letter nucleotide code could be translated into 20-letter amino acid language. The tRNA adaptor also translates the nucleotide sequence of mRNA into amino acid sequence of a polypeptide

From this, they concluded that protein synthesis by ribosomes needed the following things:
- Ribosome
- tRNA
- mRNA
- Amino acids
- Aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases
