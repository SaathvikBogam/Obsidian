Proteins are responsible for most of the membrane's functions.
Approximately 50% of membrane is composed of proteins.
The two types of proteins present in cell membrane are:
#### Integral Proteins
These cross the bilayer and strongly interact with membrane lipids through hydrophobic side chains of amino acids.
Usually are cylindrical arrays and form pores for transport of polar molecules.
They can only be removed by destroying the membrane using detergent or solvent.
#### Peripheral Proteins
These are a component of the cytoskeleton and function with receptor proteins. Associated with 2nd messenger system.
Can be removed without disturbing membrane structure by shifting $P^H$ or ionic strength slightly.
