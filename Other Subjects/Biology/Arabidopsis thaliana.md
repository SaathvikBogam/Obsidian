A. thaliana has great importance in the study of plant biology, as it is easy to cultivate and has well-established methods for genetic manipulation. It helps identify genes involved in plant development and flower formation.
Size: 30 cm in height
Lifespan: 6-8 weeks
Genome: 125M base pairs and 26000 genes (15000 unique)
##### Applications
- Cell division and development
- Cell wall biology
- Gene expression and regulation
- Plant hormone signaling
- Stress response and adaptation
