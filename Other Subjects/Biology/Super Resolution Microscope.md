The super resolution microscope was developed to over the low resolution of fluorescence microscope (200 nm), increasing to 10 $-$ 100 nm. Super resolution microscope uses fluorescent probes that can visualise cytoskeletal structures, fine details of nuclus, chromatin, cell attachment sites and plasma membrane.
#### Stochastic Optical Reconstruction Microscope (STORM)
- Resolution of 20 nm
- Only as small fraction of probes is fluorescent at any time, multiple images are obtained over time can be resolved from one another and a collective image is made.
#### Stimulated Emission Depletion (STED) Microscope
- Resolution of 30 nm
- Sample is scanned in point like in point scanning microscopy, but a focused excitation laser is surrounded by a donut shaped depletion beam which reduces excitation area
- Computer records spot's precise position, and creates a high resolution image
#### Structured Illumination Microscope (SIM)
- Resolution of 100 nm
- Specimen is illuminated with a pattern of light and dark stripes, several images are taken as illuminating pattern is rotated to generate Moire fringes
- Generates interference patterns that can be mathematically resolved to generate high resolution image
- 3D image collected every 4 seconds
