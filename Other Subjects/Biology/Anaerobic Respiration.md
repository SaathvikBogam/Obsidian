Anaerobic respiration takes place in the condition where the organism does not have oxygen to undergo aerobic respiration. Glucose is converted into pyruvate by glycolysis and some energy is released. It does not have as good of an energy output as its aerobic counterpart does. In bacteria and mammals, lactic acid fermentation occurs, whereas in yeast, ethanol fermentation occurs instead.
