NMR spectroscopy is used to analyze the structure of small molecules, proteins or protein domains. No need for crystalline sample, but requires a small volume of concentrated protein solution placed in strong magnetic field.
The principle behind NMR is the fact that nuclei have spin and are electrically charged. If external magnetic field is applied, excitation can occur, followed by de-excitation which releases EM waves of a specific wavelength/frequency. The signal that matches this transfer is measured and processed in order to yield an NMR spectrum.
The structure of proteins of about 10000 to 20000 daltons or less can be determined by this method. We can monitor changes in protein dynamics and structure during protein folding or when protein binds to another molecule. 3D structure of RNA and complex carbohydrate side chains can me imaged.
