Cancer is usually caused by tumor-promoting chemicals/radiation, hormones and sometimes viruses. Mutation in three broad classes of genes can cause cancer:
1. Proto-oncogenes: Normally promote cell growth, but mutation can change them into oncogenes.
2. Tumor-suppressor genes: Mutations in p53, Rb, etc. can inactivate these, allowing improper cell division
3. Genome maintenance genes: Required for genome's integrity, when inactivated, causes deregulation of cell growth.
The cancer forming process - onco/tumorigenesis - is an interplay between genetics and environment. Most cancers are caused by carcinogens. Cancerous cells can also acquire other properties to get oxygen. In some cases, tumors migrate to new sites, creating secondary tumors in a process called metastasis.
Tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous).
