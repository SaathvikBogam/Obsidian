Centrosome is the major microtubule organizing center in all animal cells and serves as a regulator of cell cycle progression. It consists of a pair of cylindrical structures called centrioles arranged at right angles to each other. The centrioles are surrounded by a mass of protein called pericentriolar material (PCM). PCM contains proteins responsible for microtubule nucleation and anchoring. During mitosis, nuclear membrane breaks down and centrosome-nucleated microtubules interact with chromosomes to make mitotic spindle. During the S phase, centrosome duplicates and migrates to the opposite poles of each cell. 
An abberant number of centrosomes is associated usually with cancer.
