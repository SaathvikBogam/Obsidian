Mass spectrometry is a technique used to measure the mass to charge ratio of ions. It is a vital tool for identification, quantification and characterization of biomolecules. It seperates ions by $\frac{q}{m}$ ratio, is highly sensetive and can accurately determine the mass of proteins and peptides within an margin of error of 1 in a million.

A protein is first digested by a protease into smaller peptides, which are ionized and analyzed in a mass spectrometer. The results are then displayed as a mass spectrum, which is compared to a theoretical mass spectra of all known proteins. Individual tryptic peptides are indicated by a spike corresponding to their $\frac{q}{m}$ ratio.

##### Tandem Mass Spectrometry
Used to determine amino acide sequences of peptides and analyzing post translational modifications. To do that, a mixture of peptides is seperated in one mass spectrometer, while another randomly selected peptide is fragmented by collision induced dissociation of peptide bonds. These fragments, which differ by one amino acid each, are seperated in a second mass spectrometer. Protein translation can be identified due to them changing the mass of amino acid.

##### Matrix Assisted Laser Desorption/Ionization Time of Flight (MALDI-TOF)
Used to determine molecular mass of proteins. Pulses of light ionize a protein/peptide mixture that is absorbed on a metal target, then an electric field accelerates the ions towards detector and the time of flight is proportional to $\sqrt{ \frac{m}{q} }$.
##### Electrospray Ionization (ESI)
ESI generates ions of intact molecules without any fragmentation, and can be used to analyze polar, non-volatile and thermally labile compounds. It converts proteins and peptides in a solution to highly charged gaseous ions by passing the solution through a needle that has a high voltage across it. Evaporation of solvent produces gaseous ions that are analyzed by a ion-trap mass analyzer.
##### Liquid Chromatography-Tandem Mass Spectrometry (LC-MS)
It involves the physical seperation of target compounds followed by mass based detection. Identifying and quantifying compunds in complex mixtures, particularly in fields such as pharmaceuticals, including drug metabolism, environmental monitoring, food safety and clinical diagnostics.
