Lysosomes are membrane-bound organelles that contain 50 different degradative enzymes and acid hydrolases capable of breaking down all types of biological polymers.
The glycocalyx of a lysosome is a layer of oligosaccharides that
protects the lysosome's phospholipid bilayer from hydrolytic
destruction. The glycocalyx is made up of oligosaccharides from
lysosomal membrane glycoproteins
They degrade material taken up from outside the cell and digest obsolete components of the cell itself.
