Prokaryotes are classified into gram positive and negative based on the nature of cell envelope.
The gram is the outer layer made of LPS (lipopolysaccharide).
Process of checking gram positivity:
- Add crystal violet dye to sample
- Add iodine
- Add alcohol
- Add safranin dye
At the end, the one's which are dyed red by the safranin are G- and the one's that are purple are G+. The alcohol dissolves the outer lipid rich layer and allows it to be dyed in G-.
