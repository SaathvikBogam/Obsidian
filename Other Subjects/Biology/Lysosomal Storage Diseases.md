Mutations in the genes that encode these enzymes causes more than 30 different human genetic diseases, which are called lysosomal storage diseases, like:
• *Gaucher’s disease*, caused by a mutation in the gene that encodes a lysosomal enzyme required for the breakdown of glycolipids.
• *I-cell disease*, caused by a deficiency in the enzyme that catalyzes the first step in the tagging of lysosomal enzymes with mannose-6-phosphate in the Golgi apparatus.
