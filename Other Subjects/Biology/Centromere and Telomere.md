Centromere and Telomere are two parts found at the middle and end of chromosome respectively.
The centromere is a chromosomal region where the kinetochore (protein complex) is formed, which is the attachment point of spindle fibres. It is responsible for correct chromosome segregation during cell division.
Telomeres protect chromosomal ends against enzymatic degradation, DNA damage and fusions.
