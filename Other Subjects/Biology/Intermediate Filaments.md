There are 4 types of intermediate filaments: nuclear, vimentin-like, epithelial and axonal.

| Type of I.F.  | Component Polypeptides                                        | Cellular Location                                                   |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Nuclear       | lamins A, B and C                                             | nuclear lamina                                                      |
| Vimentin-like | vimentin, desmin, peripherin, glial fibrillary acidic protein | many cells of mesenchymal origin, muscle, some neurons, glial cells |
| Epithelial    | Type I keratins (acidic), Type II keratins (basic)            | epithelial cells and their derivatives (hair and nails)             |
| Axonal        | neurofilament proteins (NF-L, NF-M and NF-H)                  | neurons                                                             |
A major function of cytoplasmic I.F. is to resist mechanical stress. Mutation in keratin genes (called human genetic disease/epidermolysis bullosa simplex) make them sensitive to mechanical injury. Even a light squeeze can cause blistering of the skin.
