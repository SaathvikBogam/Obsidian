The glyoxylate cycle is a series of reactions which convert stored fatty acids in seeds to carbohydrates in order to provide energy for germination and growth. The peroxisome which this cycle takes place in is called glyoxysome. This cycle allows seeds to use lipids as a source of energy to form the shoot during germination, since the seed cannot produce biomass using photosynthesis.
