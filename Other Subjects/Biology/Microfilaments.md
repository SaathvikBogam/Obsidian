Microfilaments are made of actin, each subunit being a monomer with a site for ATP binding site. The actin filament contains two parallel protofilaments that twist around each other in a right-handed helix. They are relatively more flexible compared to microtubules. Actin is responsible for pseudopodia in amoeba, filopodia (nerve cell axons extend) and lamellipodium (thin sheet extension of fibroblast cells).
