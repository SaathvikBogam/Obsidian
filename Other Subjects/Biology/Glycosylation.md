It is a process by which a carbohydrate is attached to a macro-molecule, usually a protein or lipid. It takes place in the lumen of ER and in the Golgi body. N-linked glycosylation begins in the ER and continues in the Golgi body, whereas O-linked glycosylation occurs exclusively in the Golgi body.
The 14-sugar-residue precursor is attached to a dolichol phosphate intermediate, and then transferred as a block to a specific asparagine residue of growing polypeptide chain.
##### Role of Glycosylation:
- Protein folding $\rightarrow$ Oligosaccharides are used as tags to mark protein folding state 
- Resistance to proteases
- Trapping of pathogens
- Intestinal lubrication
- Inter-cellular communication
