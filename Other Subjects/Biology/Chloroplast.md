The chloroplast is the photosynthetic organelle, present in all photosynthesizing life. It has three distinct enzymes: outer, inner and thylakoid membrane. The thylakoid membrane consists of the energy generating systems of the chloroplast, including the chlorophyll.
The chloroplast is a member of the Plastid family of organelles, which perform photosynthesis, store starch and synthesize other biomolecules.

In 1937, Robert Hill found leaf extracts containing chloroplasts when illuminated, they evolved $O_{2}$ or reduced a non-biological electron acceptor added to the medium. The Hill reagent (dye, 2,6-dichlorophenolindophenol) is blue when oxidized ($A$) and colorless when reduced ($AH_{2}$). He noticed that when illuminated, the blue reagent turns colorless and $O_{2}$ was evolved. In the dark, this did not occur. This was the first evidence that light causes electron flow from $H_{2}O$ to electron acceptor, later discovered to be $NADP^{+}$.
