Microtubules are formed from protein subunits of $\alpha$ and $\beta$ tubulin, which form a heterodimer tightly bound by non-covalent bonds. The $\alpha$ and $\beta$ monomer has a binding site for GTP. They are polar structures with _$-$ve_ end stuck to centrosome and **+ve** end emanating out. The position of cell organelles is determined by microtubule motors, kinesins which move toward **+ve** end (anterograde) and dyneins which move toward _$-$ve_ end (retrograde).  Kinesins transport to ER and dyneins to golgi body.
