• Most plant and fungal cells contain one or several very large, vesicles called vacuoles, surrounded by a membrane called the tonoplast. They typically occupy cell volume in the range of 30 - 90%.
• Vacuoles contains a variety of hydrolytic enzymes like lysosomes in animal cells. Plants do not contain lysosomes.
• Vacuoles help plant cells to withstand variations in their environment. When pH in the environment drops, flux of H+ into the cytosol from the vacuole to keep the pH in the cytosol constant.
• Stores pigments in plants: For example, anthocyanin pigments are stored in vacuoles to color the petals of many flowers to attract pollinating insects, while noxious molecules are released from vacuoles provide a defense against predators.
• Protein degradation: The vacuole contains enzymes that break down damaged, excess, and old proteins and organelles. This process is especially important when yeast cells are under nutrient stress.
• Storage: The vacuole stores ions, amino acids, carbohydrates, phosphate and waste material.
• Buffering: The vacuole recycles ions, amino acids, and metals back into the cytosol. The vacuole's storage and recycling functions help maintain ion and amino acid homeostasis.
