Mitotic spindle is the target of a variety of drugs that act by interfering with exchange of tubulin subunits between microtubules and free tubulin pool.
- Colchicine : Binds to a tubulin molecule and prevents polymerization into microtubules. Causes rapid disappearance of mitotic spindle.
- Vinblastine and Vincristine : Used in cancer treatment
- Taxol : Has the opposite effect by binding tightly to stabilize microtubules. It arrests division of cell in mitosis, and is widely used as an anti-cancer drug.
