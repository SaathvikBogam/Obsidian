Mitochondria is the powerhouse of the cell. It is a membrane-bound organelle, and the number of mitochondria in a cell can widely vary (liver, for example, has more than 2000 mitochondria). The number is regulated by a balance of fission, fusion and mitophagy. Without the mitochondria, animal cells would be dependent on anaerobic glycolysis for ATP generation. Aerobic respiration produces 15$\times$ more energy than anaerobic.
Bacteria do not possess mitochondria. Genome of mitochondria is similar to that of bacteria. The inner membrane is highly specialized. Its lipid bilayer contains a high proportion of "double" phospholipid cardiolipin, which has 4 fatty acids.

According to the chemiosmotic hypothesis, the action of ATP synthase is coupled with that of a proton gradient. According to this, protons are pumped across the inner mitochondrial membrane as electrons move through the electron transfer chain. The gradient creates a proton motive force that allows ATP synthase to phosphorylate ADP by inorganic phosphate to form ATP.
