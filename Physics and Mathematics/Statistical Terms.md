#### Average/Expectation Value
It is the value one could come to expect when taking a singular measurement. In the case of the wave function $\Psi$, any measurement immediately after the first will be the same as the earlier, since the $\Psi$ collapses. Therefore, the expectation value is the average of measurements performed on particles all in the state $\Psi$, not the average of the results obtained from repeated measurement. 
$$
\langle x \rangle =\sum_{x=0}^{\infty}xP(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x \rho(x)\ dx
$$
$$
\langle f(x) \rangle=\sum_{x=0}^{\infty}f(x)P(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}f(x)\rho(x)\ dx
$$
#### Variance and Standard Deviation
The numerical measure of the "spread" in a distribution is its variance. The standard deviation is its square root, which represents the average deviation from the average of the distribution.
$$
v=\sigma^2=\langle (\Delta x)^2 \rangle =\langle x^2 \rangle -\langle x \rangle
$$
