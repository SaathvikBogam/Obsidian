The wave function $\Psi$ of a particle does not have any meaning, for it is a complex function in space and time. However, Max Born proposed that the amplitude squared of the wave function $|\Psi|^2$  represents the *probability density* of the particle. Therefore, this proposal is called the Born/Statistical Interpretation of the wave function. By this interpretation, we can write the probability to find a particle with wave function $\Psi(x,t)$ between $x=a$ and $x=b$ as:
$$
P_{ab}=\int_{a}^{b}|\Psi(x,t)|^2 \,dx
$$
