The wave equation is a classical equation in second order partial derivatives that every wave must follow.
$$
\frac{\partial^2\psi}{\partial t^2}=v^2\frac{\partial ^2\psi}{\partial x^2}
$$
From this, and other boundary conditions of the problem, we can arrive at other wave equations like the Schrödinger Wave Equation.

The superposition principle also is applicable here. So if two waves $\psi_{1}(x,t)$ and $\psi_{2}(x,t)$ are solutions to the wave equation, then $\psi_{1}(x,t)+\psi_{2}(x,t)$ is also a solution.
