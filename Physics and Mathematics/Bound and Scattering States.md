Consider a classical potential $V(x)$ with maximum energy $E$. Since the energy cannot go higher than $E$, the potential will be bounded at a maximum equal to the total energy. If you imagine a cart rolling down, starting with maximum potential energy, and climb up again, it does not have enough energy to climb up, and turns back. The point where this happens is called a *turning point*.
![[Pasted image 20260331194531.png]]

If, however, the particle is free to take any potential energy within the domain of its potential energy function, it is called a scattering state.
![[Pasted image 20260331194910.png]]

In Quantum Mechanics however, the phenomenon of tunneling can allow a particle to leak through any finite potential barrier. Therefore, the only thing that matters is the potential at infinity.
$$
\begin{cases}
E<V(-\infty)\ \text{and}\ V(+\infty)\implies \text{bound state}\\E>V(-\infty)\ \text{and}\ V(+\infty)\implies \text{scattering state}
\end{cases}
$$
