X-Ray Crystallography is used to discover the 3D structure of molecules at atomic resolutions. A narrow beam of X-rays ${(}\lambda=0.1\text{ nm})$ is fired at a sample of pure protein, most of the X-rays pass through, and those which are scattered by the protein crystal reinforce each other through superposition at certain points and appear as diffraction spots. The diffraction pattern is analyzed and a 3D atomic model is made.
