DNA found in the center of a prokaryotic cell is nucleoid. It is not surrounded by a nuclear membrane and has an irregular shape.
It contains DNA, RNA and proteins unlike nucleus in eukaryotes.
The DNA is supercoiled, large and in a circular chromosome.
Bacterial DNA is folded into loops with the help of DNA-binding proteins called nucleoid-associated proteins (NAPs).
