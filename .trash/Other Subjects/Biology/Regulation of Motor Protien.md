In fish, melanocytes are responsible for rapid changes in skin colouration. They contain large pigment granules that can alter location in response to a neuronal or hormonal stimulus. Movement of pigment occurs along microtubules and can be controlled by the fish to change its skin colour. To darken, kinesins move the pigment to skin and lightening is done by dyneins moving pigment away from skin.
