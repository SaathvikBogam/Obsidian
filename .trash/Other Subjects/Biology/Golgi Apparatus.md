Golgi apparatus plays a central role in processing, modifying, and packaging proteins and lipids for transport within the cell or secretion outside the cell.
It is prominent in cells specialized for secretion such as goblet cells of epithelium of intestine, which secrete mucus, a mixture of glycoproteins synthesized in ER and golgi apparatus. It is highly polarized, with apical domain facing lumen of gut and basolateral domain facing basal lamina. The golgi apparatus is also highly polarized and secretes mucus by exocytosis of apical domain.
