The inner product is a generalization of the dot product that can be applied to vectors where we don't have a definite "length" or "direction".
Given two vectors $v,u\in V$, their inner product is denoted by $\langle v,u \rangle$. As it is a generalization of the dot product, it obeys the following properties:
- $\langle v,u \rangle=\langle u,v \rangle^{*}$
- $\langle v,v \rangle > 0\,\,;\,\langle v,v \rangle=0\,\,\text{if}\,\,v=0$
- $\langle v,au+bw \rangle=a\langle v,u \rangle+b \langle v,w\rangle\,\,;\,\,a,b\in \mathbf{C}$

A vector space with an inner product is called an **Inner Product Space**.
A few more terms related to inner products are given below:
- Two vectors are said to be *orthogonal* if their inner product vanishes.
- $\|v\|$ is the *norm* of a vector, defined as $\|v\|=\sqrt{\langle v,v\rangle}$.
- A set of basis orthogonal vectors with all of them having unit norm is called an *orthonormal* basis.
